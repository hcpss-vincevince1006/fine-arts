Enrichment Through the Fine Arts

"The arts are fundamental resources through which the world is viewed, meaning is created, and the mind developed."

- Elliot W Eisner, Professor of Education and Art, Stanford University

Overview

The fine arts (theatre, visual art, music and dance) are among humanity’s greatest achievements. The arts communicate a broad range of customs, beliefs, ideas and feelings; they are the primary means of transmitting culture throughout history, influencing an enormous range of human activity and discovery. As such the arts are an essential part of every child’s learning and cognitive development, sharpening analytical skills, encouraging abstract associations, innovative thinking, and creative problem solving.

Through the active study of the fine arts, it is our hope and belief that students, supported by a rigorous and innovative environment, develop a genuine desire for the highest level of achievement.

HCPSS Long Term Transfer Goals

Students will be able to independently:

  • Ask questions and pursue multiple pathways to generate new ideas and seek unique solutions to problems. Inquiry / Innovation
  • Value uncertainty, change, and risk taking as opportunities to explore, identify, and refine solutions to problems. Application / Process
  • Work effectively with, and learn from others in a variety of situations, from diverse backgrounds, and different perspectives, in school and beyond. Collaboration
  • Manage goals and time, embrace, analyze, and reflect upon failure, in order to move forward and ensure continuous growth / improvement. Perseverance
  • Effectively exchange information for different purposes and varied audiences using appropriate formats. Communication
